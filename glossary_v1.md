DCE Glossary v1.0
Decision Continuity Engineering — Canonical Definitions

A
Anchor Decision
A decision that stabilizes a sequence of downstream decisions by providing a fixed reference point. Anchors reduce drift and prevent re‑evaluation loops.

Authority Inheritance
The rule set that determines who or what can extend, modify, or branch from a decision capsule.

C
Capsule (Decision Capsule)
A structured container that preserves a decision’s intent, constraints, and lineage across time and context shifts. Capsules prevent silent reinterpretation.

Capsule Drift
The gradual erosion or distortion of a decision’s original meaning when not protected by a capsule.

Continuity Object
Any decision, principle, or artifact that persists across contexts while retaining identity.

Continuity Substrate
The deeper layer of stable decisions and principles that shape future decision‑making. The Root Phase integrates decisions into this substrate.

D
Decision Decay
The natural degradation of a decision’s clarity, intent, or constraints over time.

Decision Lineage
The traceable ancestry of a decision, showing how it evolved from earlier capsules or seeds.

Decision Root
A decision that has completed the Root Phase and now forms part of the continuity substrate.

Decision Seed
A new decision generated from a capsule that inherits the original intent and constraints.

E
Explicit Override
A deliberate, documented modification to a capsule’s inherited constraints or intent.

I
Intent Preservation
The principle that the original purpose of a decision must remain intact unless explicitly overridden.

Intent Statement
A concise declaration of what a decision is trying to achieve at the moment of Lock‑In.

L
Lineage Integrity
The requirement that all changes to a decision must be traceable back to the original capsule.

Lock‑In Phase
The phase where a decision’s intent, constraints, and rationale are captured before decay begins.

M
Meaning Drift
A form of decision decay where the interpretation of a decision changes without explicit modification.

P
Preservation Boundary
The set of elements within a decision that must remain unchanged across all future contexts.

R
Rationale
The reasoning behind a decision at the moment of Lock‑In. Used to detect drift and validate future branches.

Root Phase
The phase where a decision becomes part of the continuity substrate and influences future decisions.

S
Seed Phase
The phase where a capsule generates new decisions that inherit its intent and constraints.

Stability Horizon
The expected duration over which a decision remains valid before requiring re‑evaluation.

T
Traceability Requirement
The rule that all decision changes must be documented and linked to their originating capsule.
